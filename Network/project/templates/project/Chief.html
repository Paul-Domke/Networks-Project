DDoS Protection

How Can Organizations Protect Themselves?

To defend against DDoS attacks, there are a number of countermeasures that can be implemented. One method of protection is simply to increase the amount of bandwidth in a network, allowing it to handle a larger volume of requests. This will make it harder for attackers to overwhelm the network. This method is not foolproof, because of the sheer size of some attacks, but it can buy an organization more time to deploy countermeasures during an attack.

Another way to mitigate DDoS attacks is to add redundancy to a system. If attackers can bring down one part of the system, it is important to have other parts that can take over and continue providing service. Having a bottleneck or single point of failure can be a shortcoming that can make a web service quite vulnerable to DDoSing, so it is also important to distribute servers geographically.

There are also many companies that offer services that provide defense against DDoS attacks. The first role that this service plays is detecting when an attack is occurring by searching for unusual web traffic. Once an attack is detected, traffic is redirected and scrubbed - traffic that is deemed malicious is removed, while traffic attributed to actual users is allowed to pass through.

How Can You Protect Yourself?

A good way for an ordinary consumer to protect themselves from DDoS attacks is with a VPN. As well as concealing the true origin of what you transmit over the internet and thus protecting your devices from being attacked, some VPNs have anti-DDoS security features built in. It is also essential to avoid suspicious links, emails, and websites, and to install anti-virus systems and keep your software up to date. This will help prevent your computer from becoming part of a DDoS botnet.
